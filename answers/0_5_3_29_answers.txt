15.
1. Ensure the scene is safe for you and the injured person; use gloves/PPE if available.  
2. Obtain the injured person’s consent before providing aid.  
3. Assess the injury and check for immediate life threats (airway, breathing, circulation); if present, call 911 and Campus Public Safety immediately.  
4. For minor injuries, provide basic first aid: control bleeding, clean and dress wounds, immobilize sprains, apply ice for swelling, and monitor vital signs.  
5. Use a campus first aid kit or Automated External Defibrillator (AED) if required and trained to do so.  
6. Advise the person to seek follow-up care at campus health services or a physician if needed.  
7. Report the incident to Campus Public Safety (use the campus emergency/non-emergency numbers) and complete any required incident report.  
8. Document what you observed and the care given, and notify relevant supervisors or campus support services as appropriate.

17.
1. Ensure the scene is safe for you and the victim; remove or avoid hazards.  
2. Call emergency services (911) and the Campus Public Safety emergency number immediately.  
3. Give clear details: exact location, nature of the emergency, number and condition of injured persons, hazards present, and your callback number.  
4. If trained, begin appropriate first aid/CPR and use an AED if needed; control severe bleeding with direct pressure.  
5. Do NOT move an unconscious, neck- or spine-injured, or seriously injured person unless there is imminent danger.  
6. Stay with the person, monitor breathing and responsiveness, and provide reassurance until responders arrive.  
7. When responders arrive, brief them on what happened and any care you provided.  
8. Report the incident to Campus Public Safety, complete any required incident forms, and access campus support services as needed.
