If >70–80 units, apply ASAP and you may need a Work/Study plan. If >90 units, it’s often too late but consult staff. Mandatory co-op students are auto-enrolled. Review refund policy before applying. Staff will contact you within 10 business days with conditional approval and faculty-specific next steps.

4. After acceptance: remain in classes; be registered in a prerequisite Canvas course; complete three required workshops (resume, cover letter, interview); gain access to co-op job postings; apply and interview; take a 4- or 8-month co-op practicum (tuition still charged); a mid-term site visit will be arranged; many students start their first work term with ~45–60 credits.

5. Unconventional/co-op variations: international co-op; research co-op terms for certain engineering programs (ENG, MSE, SEE); Beedie CPA-eligible work terms; SFSS executive roles may count; Entrepreneurship Co-op (eCoop); student-developed work terms (new employer approval required); NCAA student pathways; co-op for several post-undergraduate programs.
