Signs and symptoms of critical incident stress
- Emotional: anxiety, fear, anger, guilt, sadness, numbness, mood swings, tearfulness
- Cognitive: confusion, memory problems, difficulty concentrating, intrusive thoughts, disorientation
- Physical: fatigue, sleep disturbance or insomnia, headaches, gastrointestinal upset, rapid heartbeat, sweating
- Behavioral: withdrawal or isolation, irritability, agitation, changes in appetite, increased use of alcohol or drugs, hypervigilance
- Social/relational: difficulty maintaining relationships, conflict, reduced interest in usual activities
- Spiritual/existential: loss of meaning, shaken beliefs or worldview
