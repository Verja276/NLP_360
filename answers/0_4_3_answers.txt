The Co-op Abroad Co-op Readiness Checklist is a list of steps and requirements to ensure you are prepared and "good to go" for an international co-op placement.

Review it by opening the Co-op Abroad Readiness Checklist link and working through each item — meet with your Co-op Program Coordinator, sign up for the Co-op Abroad email list and info sessions, prepare an international-style resume and cover letter, and complete any other pre-departure requirements listed.

11.
- Research country-specific resume/CV formats and conventions.  
- Adapt your resume layout and content to local norms (length, photo, personal details, date formats, contact info).  
- Highlight relevant international experience, language skills, and cross-cultural competencies.  
- Tailor your cover letter to explain your motivation, fit for the role, and what you bring internationally.  
- Get feedback from your Co-op Program Coordinator or career services and use SFU’s international co-op resources (readiness checklist, guides).

13.
- Clear statement of the position and location you are applying for  
- Brief opening that explains why you’re interested in this international co-op  
- Key relevant skills, experiences, and achievements tailored to the role  
- Examples of how your experience matches the employer’s needs (quantified when possible)  
- Language skills and proficiency levels  
- Cross-cultural experience, adaptability, and ability to work internationally  
- Awareness of and alignment with country-specific workplace expectations or customs  
- Availability dates and duration you can work abroad  
- Visa/permit eligibility or willingness to obtain necessary documentation  
- Contact information and a polite call to action/closing statement
