A Rapid Access/Intake Appointment is a 30-minute meeting with a mental health clinician where you complete a brief questionnaire beforehand; the clinician will ask questions to clarify your needs, review your questionnaire, help you identify a goal, and develop an action plan that may include referrals or resources.

To schedule one, call the front desk at 778-782-4615 and ask to schedule an intake appointment (availability permitting you will be matched with the next available clinician within two business days). Appointments can also be booked in person and up to two business days in advance; complete the brief questionnaire before your appointment.

A single-session counselling follow-up is a 30‑ or 50‑minute in‑person or remote appointment after your rapid access/intake where the clinician works with you to identify a detailed goal and action plan and may refer you to short‑term counselling, group programs, or longer‑term community services.

You might receive it to follow up on concerns raised at intake, for example to manage academic stress or exam anxiety, address relationship issues with parents or a partner, cope with a recent break‑up, or learn skills to enhance mood or sleep.

What are group-based programs?
- Programs delivered in a group format that teach skills, provide coping strategies, and offer peer-based support for shared concerns.

What are their benefits?
- Help develop practical skills
- Assist with managing distress
- Build social connections and reduce isolation
- Provide collective, compassionate support
- Can be as effective or more effective than individual counselling
