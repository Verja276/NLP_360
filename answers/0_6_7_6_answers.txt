Who SVSPO supports
- Confidential support and guidance for any SFU and FIC community member impacted by or witnessing sexual violence, or supporting someone who has.

How to access support
- Contact a case manager by phone or email (above). For other options, visit the SVSPO Contact Us page.
- Free, on‑demand workshops and outreach are available via Request a Workshop.

Responding to a disclosure — key actions
- Listen: respond with compassion; avoid prying questions.
- Believe: validate feelings and state it is not their fault.
- Empower: ask what they need and offer to connect them to resources.
- Safeguard privacy: do not share identifying information without explicit consent; an SVSPO case manager can advise without needing the student’s identity.
- Refer/report: if they want to report to SFU, refer to SVSPO to guide them through options.
- De-brief: consult an SVSPO case manager for guidance, resources, and personal support.

Classroom incidents
- Address behaviour/language by noting it does not meet syllabus expectations; invite the individual to follow up outside class if desired.
- Remind the class of support resources if appropriate.
- Consult OSSRR, the Centre for Educational Excellence (CEE), and SVSPO for guidance.

Disclosures in assignments or group discussions
- Treat seriously and consult SVSPO/appropriate offices for guidance and support (specific protocols depend on context).

Content notes (content warnings)
- Purpose: give advance notice of potentially distressing material related to sexual violence or other trauma.
- Use cases: general course content or specific readings/assignments.
- Elements to include (based on examples): identify the topics/content that may be distressing; acknowledge potential impact; state when the material will be discussed; invite students to seek confidential support; provide support resources and links; offer alternative ways to achieve the same learning outcome.
- Example supports to list:
  - SFU’s Sexual Violence Support & Prevention Office: https://www.sfu.ca/sexual-violence.html
  - SFU Health & Counselling Services: https://www.sfu.ca/students/health/

Policy and resources
- SFU’s Sexual Violence and Misconduct Prevention, Education, and Support Policy (GP 44) applies.
- The SVSPO co‑authored resources with the Centre for Educational Excellence (see blog on content notes).
- A case manager can guide reporting options and confidentiality questions.
