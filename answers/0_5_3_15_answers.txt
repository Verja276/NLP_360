12.
- Book a 1-on-1 Career Educator appointment on myExperience (select by date, topic or educator) or check drop-in schedules for shorter sessions.
- Bring a digital or physical copy of your resume/CV and the job posting to every appointment.
- Ask a Career Educator for a referral if you want a Mock Interview (note: mock interviews are by referral and must be booked through the Front Desk, not myExperience).
- Use a 50-minute 1-on-1 session (in-person or Zoom) to get targeted feedback on answers, body language, and interview strategy.
- Get your resume/CV and cover letter reviewed beforehand so interview feedback focuses on interview performance rather than application basics.
- Research the employer and role: mission, products/services, recent news, required skills, and typical interview formats (behavioral, technical, case, panel).
- Prepare and practice STAR stories (Situation, Task, Action, Result) that demonstrate key skills and achievements relevant to the role.
- Practice common and role-specific questions aloud, record yourself or do a practice video call to check pacing, tone, and body language.
- Prepare questions to ask the interviewer and plan brief closing statements about fit and enthusiasm.
- For virtual interviews, test your Zoom setup: camera, microphone, lighting, background, and internet; have a backup device and charger ready.
- Bring multiple printed copies of your resume, a list of references, a notepad and pen, and any portfolio samples or work artifacts if relevant.
- Arrive early (or log in 10–15 minutes before online interviews), dress professionally, and have contact info for the Career Centre (778-782-3106, careers@sfu.ca) handy if needed.
