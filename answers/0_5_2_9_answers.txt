Co-operative Education is an educational model that formally integrates academic study with relevant, paid work experience. The standard Co-op program consists of periods of academic study combined with periods of work experience in appropriate employment fields such as business, industry, government, the professions, not-for-profit and community-based organizations.

Co-op students can apply for government student financial assistance (loans and grants).
A Co-op work term is not considered the same as having a full‑time job for student financial assistance.

4.
Co-operative Education integrates academic study with relevant paid work experience in fields like business, industry, government, professions, not‑for‑profit and community organizations.

Students in a Co-op work term can apply for government student financial assistance (loans and grants).

A Co-op work term is not treated the same as having a full‑time job for student financial assistance applications.

Enrolment in a Co-op work term is considered equivalent to full‑time study; students may apply for In‑Study Payment‑Free (In‑Study, Interest‑Free) status during the work term — this is not automatic.

BC students applying through StudentAid BC must follow the StudentAid BC requirements (contact a Financial Aid Advisor for specifics).

Students must submit a request for confirmation of enrolment each term via their National Student Loan Service Centre (NSLSC) portal to update student status.

Contact a Financial Aid Advisor for further information.

Students enrolled in an SFU Co-op work term only are not eligible for scholarships/awards/bursaries administered by Financial Aid and Awards (the 3‑unit Co-op work term is not a standard graded course); students must meet minimum eligibility requirements.

Students enrolled in an SFU International Co‑op work term are eligible to apply for SFU International Co‑op Education Bursaries and/or SFU International Co‑op Education Awards.

Contact: fiassist@sfu.ca
