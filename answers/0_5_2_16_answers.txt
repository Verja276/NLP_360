1.
A safe, low-barrier, non‑judgmental community fridge at SFU that builds and enhances food security by allowing staff, students, faculty and community members to take or leave food to support each other and reduce food waste.

2.
- Build and enhance food security for the SFU community
- Provide a safe, low-barrier, non-judgmental way for people at SFU to access food
- Reduce food waste by enabling donations from individuals, campus food services, and local grocers
- Enable staff, students, and faculty to support each other and ensure everyone can feed themselves and their families

3.
- Anyone can take food if they need it — no questions asked.  
- If you have extra food, leave it in the fridge for someone else.  
- It’s a nonjudgmental, low‑barrier system to support each other and reduce food waste.  
- The fridge is not policed; people are not monitored for how much they take.  
- Prepared food donations must meet safety requirements (made in a licensed commercial kitchen and properly labelled with ingredients, date, and contact).
