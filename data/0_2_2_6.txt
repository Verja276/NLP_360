Parents and Guardians


Scholarships recognize outstanding academic achievement.

Bursaries are awarded on the basis of a demonstrated financial need.

BC and non-BC residents can apply to a variety of government assistance, including loans, grants, and student lines of credit.

Awards acknowledge outstanding achievements or contributions to the community.

The work-study program offers paid opportunities on campus for full-time students.

The SFU Service Awards recognize outstanding service to the University and community, leadership and volunteer work.

Students participating in a Co-op term are eligible to apply for  government-issued, student financial assistance.

Students participating in an Exchange or Field School offered through SFU’s International Services for Students (ISS) may be eligible for different types of funding

The Undergraduate Open Scholarship recognizes and supports undergraduate students who are highly qualified academically and awards scholarships to students on a term by term basis.

Students with disabilities are eligible to receive institutionally administered financial aid funding if they meet course enrolment and eligibility requirements.

Students living – or who have lived – as youth in care in BC, may qualify for financial support to cover some educational costs.

Students enrolled in a partnership program have access to the same funding sources as any other eligible SFU program.
